One Health - One World

Hygiene, vaccination, and biodiversity all play crucial roles in maintaining health. Hygiene, like handwashing, is a cost-effective way to prevent the spread of infectious diseases, while vaccination protects against specific illnesses by stimulating the immune system. Biodiversity, the variety of life on Earth, provides essential ecosystem services like clean water and food, and also plays a role in regulating disease and climate, impacting human health.

    Hygiene's role

    Reduces infection spread:

    Good hygiene practices, like handwashing with soap, are highly effective in preventing the transmission of various infectious diseases, including diarrhea, pneumonia, and other illnesses. These practices are particularly important in settings where healthcare is delivered, such as when administering vaccines.

    Cost-effective:

    Hygiene interventions are relatively inexpensive and can significantly impact public health.

    Impacts community health:

    Improved hygiene in schools and communities can lead to fewer illnesses and improved learning outcomes.

    Vaccination

    Prevents diseases:

    Vaccines protect against a wide range of diseases, including measles, polio, and influenza, saving millions of lives annually.

    Builds community immunity:

    Vaccination helps achieve herd immunity, protecting individuals who cannot be vaccinated.

    Economic benefits:

    Vaccines reduce healthcare costs and improve productivity by preventing illnesses and disability.

    Biodiversity

    Ecosystem health:

    Biodiversity ensures the stability and resilience of ecosystems, which are crucial for providing essential resources and services.

    Disease prevention:

    Loss of biodiversity can increase the risk of zoonotic diseases and other health problems.

    Resource availability:

    Biodiversity contributes to food security, water purification, and other essential resources.
