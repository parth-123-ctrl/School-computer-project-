One Health - One World

"One Health, One World" is a concept emphasizing the interconnectedness of human, animal, and environmental health, advocating for a holistic approach to address global health challenges. It recognizes that the health of one is linked to the health of the other, requiring collaboration across sectors to achieve better health outcomes.

Why does One Health matter? - One Health TrustA One Health project is crucial for several reasons, including preventing and controlling diseases that spread between animals and humans (zoonotic diseases), addressing antimicrobial resistance (AMR), ensuring food safety, and mitigating environmental threats to human and animal health. It also promotes global health security by strengthening surveillance, preparedness, and response to emerging health challenges.
